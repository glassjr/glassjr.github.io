Tic-Tac-Toe Reinforcement Learner

I taught a computer how to teach itself to play tic-tac-toe. Reinforcement learning is an area of machine learning inspired by behaviorist psychology often used to teach AI to play games. I used a method called Q-learning; this webpage provides a good introduction. Basically I create a map of every boardstate and connect them with the moves, or actions, that bring the game from one state to another. Reward the AI for winning and punish it for losing, and the bot eventually learns how to make good plays consistently. At first, the bot knows only that there are nine possible moves every turn; it doesn't understand that overlapping moves are illegal or even that three in-a-row wins the game. Through the Q-learning algorithm it learns the patterns that lead to a reward. In this case, making three in-a-row and while preventing the opponent from doing the same. In the future I would like to tackle a more complicated game and use deep learning to manage the Q-values. Check out the code here.


Pan Pizza

I think pan pizza is the best and easiest pizza you can make at home in a residential oven. I make just about everything from scratch: the dough, the sauce and most toppings. I use store-bought low-moisture mozzarella, though I hear homemade mozzarella is not that hard. My goto pizzas are classic pepperoni and white sauce with caramelized onions. Recipe coming soon on my blog.


Sourdough Bread

I love sandwiches, and sourdough is my favorite sandwich bread, so I made some from scratch. It's a labor of love and at times felt like having a pet around. You have this gooey mess of flour, water, and natural yeast brewing in your pantry that you have to feed a few times a week. I adapted this recipe to suit my tastes/environment. After a few weeks of maintaining a starter, I was churning out beautiful, tasty loaves on the reg. Recipe coming soontm on my blog.
